From product brief to export cartons, one accountable manufacturing path.

Project Workflow

Clear stages reduce back-and-forth and bad quotations.

01

Brief intake

Buyer confirms target market, channel, product format, volume, packaging direction, and document needs.

02

Sample plan

YUJI proposes existing structures or custom development scope, then prepares samples for buyer review.

03

Artwork & approval

Packaging, instruction leaflet, labels, carton marks, and product details are approved before production.

04

Production & release

Production follows first-article approval, process sampling, final inspection, document pack, and export handoff.

Customization Surface

What buyers can reasonably customize.

Customization depends on product type, volume, mold status, package complexity, and the claims allowed in the target market.

Product

Size, capacity concept, color, hardness, rim feel, formula direction, accessory mix.

Packaging

Storage pouch, retail box, leaflet, wrapper, starter kit, outer carton, barcode and label.

Documentation

Specification sheet, available certificate scans, QC records, test summary, sterilization-related documents.

Channel

Amazon, DTC, distributor catalog, pharmacy pilot, regional retail, sample kits, and mixed bundle packs.

Information needed for an accurate quote

  • Target market and sales channel
  • Product category and expected quantity
  • Color, size, formula, pack, or kit requirements
  • Required certificates, reports, or compliance review
  • Sample deadline and mass-production timeline

MOQ & Lead Time

Reference numbers are useful, but the quote depends on the actual pack.

Typical trial references: 1,000 pcs for menstrual cups and 10,000 pcs for sanitary pads. Regular orders are often planned around 15-20 working days after approvals, subject to materials, packaging, volume, and document requirements.
