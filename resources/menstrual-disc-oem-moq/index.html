MOQ Guide

Menstrual disc OEM MOQ: what buyers should define before quoting.

MOQ is not a single number. For reusable menstrual discs it depends on structure, color, packaging, sample approval, document expectations, and how much launch risk the buyer wants to remove before mass production.

Last reviewed: July 9, 2026

Buyer Summary

The most useful MOQ answer starts with the launch model.

Buyers often ask for menstrual disc MOQ before they have fixed the disc format, packaging, market language, or document needs. That makes quotes hard to compare. A serious RFQ should state whether the buyer wants a fast private-label launch from an existing structure, a market test with limited packaging changes, or a more customized disc program.

StructureExisting disc options usually quote faster than new tooling or shape development.
ColorCustom colors can change material planning, approval steps, and quantity logic.
PackagingBox, pouch, leaflet, barcode, carton, and language choices affect MOQ.
DocumentsMarket-specific records and claim review should be requested before launch.

What changes menstrual disc MOQ?

  1. Disc structure. Standard rim and pull-ring options can be reviewed from available programs; new development needs more planning.
  2. Material and color. Medical-grade silicone programs need color approval and consistency checks before production.
  3. Packaging complexity. A simple pouch and leaflet is different from a retail box, multi-language insert, bundle kit, or distributor carton program.
  4. Sample approval path. Buyers should confirm fit, rim feel, removal education, packaging copy, and artwork before placing a larger order.
  5. Compliance scope. The destination market may require additional document review, label wording checks, or importer-side validation.
  6. Launch forecast. A credible reorder plan often gives the supplier a better basis for production and packaging planning.

RFQ information that produces a real quote

Send the target country, channel, preferred disc format, capacity expectation, rim feel preference, pull-ring interest, color, packaging language, pouch or box structure, sample quantity, launch date, first-order forecast, and document list. Without these fields, the supplier can only return a generic range.

RFQ fieldWhy it mattersExample buyer input
Target marketControls label language and claim riskUS DTC, English packaging
Disc formatAffects sample and tooling pathReusable disc with pull-ring option
Pack structureChanges packaging MOQ and lead timeRetail box, pouch, leaflet, barcode
Document needsPrevents late compliance surprisesSpecs, available certificate scans, QC release

Recommended path for a first disc launch

Start by comparing available disc structures, then request samples with packaging direction. After fit, rim feel, and user-education copy are reviewed, confirm artwork, document scope, and first-order forecast. This order of work reduces rework and makes MOQ negotiation more practical.
