Sourcing Guide

How to choose a menstrual cup OEM manufacturer.

Use this guide to compare suppliers by material control, product structure, sampling discipline, packaging capability, QC release, and export documentation before asking only for price.

Last reviewed: July 8, 2026

Fast supplier screen

  • Medical-grade silicone material documentation
  • S/M/L sizing and capacity references
  • Sample approval before mass production
  • Packaging and instruction leaflet support
  • QC release records and certificate scans
  • Clear RFQ response path

Procurement Criteria

Do not compare suppliers by unit price alone.

A menstrual cup is a sensitive-care product. The supplier should be able to explain material selection, size logic, hardness direction, packaging components, inspection records, and which claims still require target-market verification. A low quotation without these facts creates risk for importers and brand owners.

MaterialMedical-grade LSR silicone should be supported by available material and test documentation.
MOQYUJI uses 1,000 pcs as a trial MOQ reference for cup programs, subject to customization.
Lead timeRegular orders are often planned around 15-20 working days after approvals.
DocumentsRequest specs, certificate scans, test summaries, QC release, and packaging files.

Questions to ask before sampling

  1. Which existing cup structures are available, and which need development?
  2. What are the S/M/L dimensions, capacity references, stem style, and hardness options?
  3. Can the factory support color, logo, pouch, box, leaflet, and starter kit packaging?
  4. Which material, testing, sterilization-related, and QC records can be reviewed?
  5. What sample approval steps happen before artwork and mass production?
  6. Which claims must the buyer verify for the destination market?

Recommended RFQ message

Send target country, sales channel, cup sizes, quantity, color direction, logo placement, pouch or box requirements, instruction language, launch date, and compliance documents needed. This gives the supplier enough information to return a useful product pack instead of a generic price list.
